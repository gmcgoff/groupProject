Courses

Semester 1

Introduction to Business and Management - 3 credits
Students will be introduced to business operations and the business's responsibilities and challenges. The course emphasizes human relations, management, marketing, finance, human resources, global business, and starting a business.
Intro to Adobe Illustrator - 1 credit
Intro to Adobe Illustrator prepares the learner to use Adobe's Illustrator software to create digital art. The course will cover the basics of computer illustration and its use in print and web media.
Intro to Photoshop - 1 credit
Intro to Adobe Photoshop—Designing and Editing introduces the learner to using Adobe Photoshop to edit, enhance, and combine digital images prepared for print and web media.
Introduction to Programming - 2 credits
Introduction to Programming is designed for students with little or no programming experience. This course introduces the fundamental concepts of object-oriented programming. Students will analyze the standards of an introductory programming language and learn the standards of a simple one.
Web Servers and Web Server Administration - 2 credits
In Web Servers and Web Server Administration, students will prepare an environment for and then install and configure an open-source web server. During the remainder of the semester, they will configure their server and install services for projects.
Markup Basics 1 - 1 credit
In Markup Basics 1, students will analyze the foundation of web pages—markup languages. They will also analyze the basics of how web browsers read markup language to display web pages. Finally, students will organize text and add images or videos to create useful and engaging web pages.
Design Basics for Front-end Development 1 - 1 credit
Design Basics for Front-end Development 1 will analyze the elements of good design while learning the basics of design and style coding.
Intro to Digital Photography & Color - 1 credit
Intro to Digital Photography & Color will have students examine photographic principles and file manipulation. Students will also engage in and produce products through printing, cropping, retouching, and creating a file management system.
Suggested General Education Course(s): English Composition

Credits: 15

Semester 2

High-Level Scripting - 3 credits
High-level Scripting teaches students to apply the skills of high-level scripting. It covers syntax, variables, conditionals, and functions, focusing on scripting for the web with Javascript.
Design Basics for Front-end Development 2 - 2 credits
Design Basics 2 builds on Design Basics 1 and will further analyze design elements of web pages and evaluate more advanced level layout creation and customization with scripting.
Markup Basics 2 - 2 credits
Markup Basics 2 builds on Markup Basics 1 and will focus on creating interactive web pages, effectively showcasing tabular data, and using markup code to make content more accessible.
Relational Database Design & Development - 3 credits
Relational Database Design & Development gives students a knowledge base in database concepts for design and development. Students will design and then develop a database in a virtual environment and write code to manipulate the data in the database.
Suggested General Education Course(s): Mathematical Reasoning & Introduction to Sociology

Credits: 16-17

Semester 3

Project & Framework Management - 2 credits
In Project & Framework Management, students will evaluate various tools used in web development to plan, organize, and execute digital projects. They will demonstrate storyboarding, wireframes, and usability testing and explore the use of various project management tools.
Version Control - 2 credits
In Version Control, students explore the basics of software version control (SVC), including process management. They evaluate a brief history of SVC and analyze the fundamental concepts of check-in/checkout, forking, merging, commits, and distribution. They then create a repository and manage files within it.
Cybersecurity - 1 credit
Cybersecurity provides an overview of the importance of websites, server security awareness, and data confidentiality through the basic aspects of information security. This course introduces students to common methods of securing data and presents best practices in access control and password policies on the web.
Content Management Systems 1 - 1 credit
Content Management Systems 1 will review the many options for proprietary and open-source content management systems. Students will evaluate the systems' options and identify the best solution for different scenarios.
Content Management Systems 2 - 1 credit
In Content Management Systems 2, students will install and configure an open-source content management system, along with user training and roll out.
Search Engine Optimization - 1 credit
In Search Engine Optimization, students will identify what search engine optimization (SEO) is and why it's an important skill for front-end developers to possess. Students will analyze free and paid keyword search tools, identify keyword ranks, and generate a keyword plan.
Front End Framework - 1 credit
In Front End Framework, students will develop the skills and knowledge they need to code with the front-end framework.
Mobile Application Development - 1 credit
In Mobile Application Development, students will identify and apply learning of how mobile technology works, including tools for development, specifications, and implementation.
Emerging Technologies Research - 1 credit
In Emerging Technologies Research, students will identify a problem that technology can solve and research options for solving it using a specified programming language or web tool.

Credits: 17

Semester 4

Responsive Design - 2 credits
In Responsive Design, students analyze the mobile-first responsive design method, outlining the basics of creating websites that are functional across platforms and devices. As they become comfortable with the methods used, students will learn to code for accessibility.
Programming - 3 credits
In Programming, students will identify and apply the foundations of programming on the LAMP stack (Linux, Apache, MySQL, and PHP), from a general overview of a server-side programming language. Students will learn to program with PHP and create functional web pages with server-side scripting.
Social Media - 3 credits
In Social Media, students will explore its uses and the tools available to manage it. They will then learn about integrating social media with websites and explore the options in social media for advertising, including creating ads and identifying audiences. Finally, students will explore open Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) within social media software and build a functioning program that connects to a social media platform.
Resume and Portfolio Development for Web Developers - 1 credit
In Resume and Portfolio Development for Web Developers, students review the work they’ve completed in the program and use this review to develop a full portfolio website. From this website portfolio review, students will also engage in resume writing and employment readiness skills.
Emerging Technologies Capstone - 3 credits
In the Emerging Technologies Capstone, students will use the skills learned in the program to identify a digital solution to a complex problem. They will then outline the problem, find a solution, present their findings for peer review and collaboration.

Credits: 12

Total Credits: 60-61

General Education Courses Within Program (can be taken any semester)

English Composition 1 - 3 credits
English Composition is designed for learners to develop knowledge and skills in all aspects of the writing process, including planning, organizing, writing, editing, and revising, which are applied through various activities.
Mathematical Reasoning or Intermediate Algebra with Applications - 3-4 credits
In Mathematical Reasoning, students will develop conceptual and procedural tools to support the use of key mathematical concepts in various contexts.
Introduction to Sociology - 3 credits
Introduction to Sociology introduces students to the basic concepts of sociology: culture, socialization, social stratification, multi-culturalism, and the five institutions, including family, government, economics, religion, and education. Other topics include demography, deviance, technology, environment, social issues, social change, social organization, and workplace issues.
Speech - 3 credits
Speech explores the fundamentals of effective oral presentation to small and large groups. Topic selection, audience analysis, methods of organization, research, supporting evidence, delivery techniques, active listening, and other essential elements of speaking successfully form the basis of the course.
Introduction to Psychology - 3 credits
Introduction to Psychology surveys the multiple aspects of human behavior. It directs the student to an insightful understanding of the complexities of human relationships in personal, social, and vocational settings.
