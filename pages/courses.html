Courses

Semester 1

Introduction to Business and Management - 3 credits
Students will be introduced to business operations and the business's responsibilities and challenges. The course emphasizes human relations, management, marketing, finance, human resources, global business, and starting a business.
Intro to Adobe Illustrator - 1 credit
Intro to Adobe Illustrator prepares the learner to use Adobe's Illustrator software to create digital art. The course will cover the basics of computer illustration and its use in print and web media.
Intro to Photoshop - 1 credit
Intro to Adobe Photoshop—Designing and Editing introduces the learner to using Adobe Photoshop to edit, enhance, and combine digital images prepared for print and web media.
Introduction to Programming - 2 credits
Introduction to Programming is designed for students with little or no programming experience. This course introduces the fundamental concepts of object-oriented programming. Students will analyze the standards of an introductory programming language and learn the standards of a simple one.
Web Servers and Web Server Administration - 2 credits
In Web Servers and Web Server Administration, students will prepare an environment for and then install and configure an open-source web server. During the remainder of the semester, they will configure their server and install services for projects.
Markup Basics 1 - 1 credit
In Markup Basics 1, students will analyze the foundation of web pages—markup languages. They will also analyze the basics of how web browsers read markup language to display web pages. Finally, students will organize text and add images or videos to create useful and engaging web pages.
Design Basics for Front-end Development 1 - 1 credit
Design Basics for Front-end Development 1 will analyze the elements of good design while learning the basics of design and style coding.
Intro to Digital Photography & Color - 1 credit
Intro to Digital Photography & Color will have students examine photographic principles and file manipulation. Students will also engage in and produce products through printing, cropping, retouching, and creating a file management system.
English Composition 1 - 3 credits
English Composition is designed for learners to develop knowledge and skills in all aspects of the writing process, including planning, organizing, writing, editing, and revising, which are applied through various activities.

Credits: 15
